What guidance looks like in practice

A short exchange focused on clarifying assumptions and reasoning through decisions.

  1. Mentee brings a concrete problem.
  2. Mentor diagnoses gaps and confusion.
  3. Learning path is defined together.
  4. Mentee executes and experiments.
  5. One to one review and guidance.
  6. Results are evaluated and explained.
  7. Process iterates and deepens.

This is a continuous, iterative process based on practical work, not theory alone.

Why guidance matters today

AI can accelerate output, but it does not create understanding. Without a clear , mentees trust results they do not fully grasp.

That leads to fragile decisions and production issues when requirements or assumptions are misunderstood. Mentorship teaches how to reason before using tools, so choices stay grounded and defensible.

How I approach learning software engineering

I tend to stay with what feels unclear before moving on. The work focuses on identifying missing pieces and rebuilding understanding from simple building blocks.

This is not a branded method. It is simply how I work: plain language, reusable ideas, and one to one adapted to each person and context.
